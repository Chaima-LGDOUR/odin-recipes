Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a traditional Italian dish made with layers of flat pasta sheets, meat sauce, béchamel, and cheese, typically mozzarella or ricotta. Originating in ancient times, it has evolved into a beloved comfort food, often featuring ground beef or sausage mixed with tomatoes, garlic, and herbs. The dish is assembled in layers, creating a rich and hearty meal perfect for family gatherings or special occasions.

Modern variations of lasagna cater to different dietary preferences, including vegetarian options that replace meat with roasted vegetables like zucchini and spinach. Some recipes also incorporate béchamel sauce or pesto for added flavor. Whether baked until golden and bubbly or served fresh, lasagna continues to delight with its satisfying combination of savory flavors and textures.
